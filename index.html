The Town of Princeville

Princeville, North Carolina, located south east of Rocky Mount, is a small town recognized as the first independently governed African American community chartered in the United States. Princeville remains around 92% African American to this day and is an important cultural hub for the area. Wilson Cemetery epitomizes this rich history with gravestones dating back from the time of the towns settlement in 1865 to the present day. Due to the Princeville's low elevation along the Tar River, the town is extemely ceceptable to flooding and has sustained significant damage in recent hurricanes. This project aims to not only digitize this cemetery in the event of flood damage, but also provide an opportunity for people around the community and country to make ancestral connections to those buried in the cemetery. Our Team

▼

This project is a partnership between students and professors at the University of North Carolina Chapel Hill through UNC Rural and the talented team at Freedom Org.

Founded in 2019 at the UNC Chapel Hill, UNC Rural serves the state of North Carolina by connecting rural communities with campus partners and resources. The organization offers programs and tools to strengthen partnerships that advance rural well-being. With the support of UNC Rural, Professor of Geography Javier Arce-Nazario led a small group of students to help execute the vision of Freedom Org and community members in preserving and engaging the community of Princeville.

Freedom Org is a community development organization investing in communities that have been historically disinvested, fostering growth, empowerment, and sustainable change. They collaborate with local, state, and federal agencies in developing holistic and all-encompassing solutions for community development. Their primary focus is on historically Black and/or low-moderate income communities, with an unwavering commitment to fostering self-sufficiency and empowerment economic freedom.
